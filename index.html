Travel to Statue Of Unity (Travel Guide)

Erected in the state of Gujarat, India, this monumental tribute holds a special place in the hearts of millions. As the tallest statue in the world, it symbolizes unity, strength, and the visionary leadership of India’s first Deputy Prime Minister, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel. From its towering presence to the panoramic views, the Statue of Unity is a testament to India’s rich heritage and a marvel that must be experienced.

History

The history of the Statue of Unity is a captivating tale that traces back to the visionary idea proposed by Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The idea of constructing a monumental statue to honour Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, the “Iron Man of India,” was first proposed in 2010. The statue aimed to pay tribute to Patel’s immense contributions to the nation and promote tourism in the region. The foundation stone was laid on October 31, 2013, marking Patel’s birth anniversary. It was a massive undertaking that involved thousands of skilled workers, engineers, and artisans who tirelessly brought this ambitious project to fruition. After years of meticulous planning and construction, the Statue of Unity was unveiled on October 31, 2018, in a momentous ceremony that marked the 143rd birth anniversary of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel. Today, it stands as an enduring symbol of unity, strength, and the indomitable spirit of India.

Lesser known FACTS

The Statue of Unity, standing tall in all its grandeur, holds more surprises than meets the eye. Beyond its awe-inspiring height and striking design, there are intriguing lesser-known facts that make this monumental tribute even more fascinating. Each revelation adds to the allure and fascination surrounding this remarkable monument. This makes it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts, art admirers, and those seeking inspiration from India’s rich cultural heritage. Let’s unravel some of the hidden marvels surrounding the Statue of Unity:

  • Viewing gallery – At the chest level of the statue, there is a viewing gallery that offers panoramic vistas of the surroundings, including the stunning Vindhya and Satpura mountain ranges.
  • Star attractions – The statue is not the only attraction in the complex. Visitors can also explore the museum and exhibition halls that showcase Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel’s life, achievements and the historical events that shaped India.
  • Dynamic lighting – As the sun sets, the statue undergoes a stunning transformation. It becomes illuminated with vibrant colours, creating a mesmerizing spectacle that accentuates its magnificence.
  • Natural splendour – The statue is located on Sadhu Bet Island, near the Sardar Sarovar Dam, providing breathtaking views of the Narmada River and the lush landscapes that surround it.
  • Dedication to unity – The bronze panels used for cladding the statue were sourced from different parts of India, symbolizing the unity and diversity of the nation.
  • Inspirational walkway – Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along the beautifully landscaped walkway that leads to the statue. The path is adorned with sculptures and murals depicting significant events from Sardar Patel’s life.
  • Global recognition – The Statue of Unity has gained worldwide acclaim and has been recognized by organizations such as the World Federation of Great Towers, which awarded it the prestigious “Certificate of Excellence.”
  • Eco-friendly initiatives – The statue complex incorporates various sustainable and eco-friendly features, including solar energy panels and a wastewater treatment plant, showcasing a commitment to environmental conservation.
  • Tourism hub – The Statue of Unity has become a major tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors every year who come to witness its grandeur and pay homage to the visionary leader it represents.

Best Time to visit

The best time to visit the Statue of Unity is during the winter months, from November to February. With pleasant temperatures ranging from 10°C to 25°C (50°F to 77°F), you can comfortably explore the monument and its surrounding attractions. The cooler weather allows for a leisurely stroll through the gardens, a visit to the museum, and uninterrupted views from the observation deck. Additionally, the absence of scorching summer heat ensures a more enjoyable experience. Whether you’re captivated by the statue’s magnificence or the panoramic vistas it offers, the winter season provides the ideal backdrop for an unforgettable visit to the Statue of Unity.
